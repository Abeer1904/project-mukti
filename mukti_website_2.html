The Land
Mewat is not a romantic origin story

It is a climate-vulnerable landscape at the edge of the Aravalli range — rocky in parts, saline in others, chronically water-stressed. The IFAD programme (1995–2005) built 1,048 check dams and 248 percolation ponds here because the land was already in crisis thirty years ago. Erratic monsoons waterlog fields one season and parch them the next. Groundwater in several blocks is too saline to use. The land is difficult. The people who grow food on it are extraordinary.

28.1°N · semi-arid · Aravalli foothills · ~550mm rainfall
🌾
The Crop
What grows here is not a coincidence

Bajra, mustard, moong, arhar, guar — grown in Mewat for centuries because they are suited to this land. Drought-tolerant. Low-water. Soil-restoring. The Green Revolution pushed toward wheat and cotton, which this soil cannot sustain without inputs. Mukti builds a commercial incentive to grow what belongs here. The biryani masala starts with mustard grown in the same district as the woman who blends it.

Primary crops: mustard, bajra, moong, arhar, guar · all drought-resilient
🪨
The Soil
Salinity is not a metaphor

Soil electrical conductivity in affected blocks exceeds 4 dS/m — the threshold above which most crops show yield stress. Saline water rises through capillary action in dry seasons. Waterlogging deposits more salt in wet ones. The soil is not broken — it is stressed, and stress can be reversed. Mukti's reinvestment fund targets gypsum application, green manuring, and drainage on supplier farms. Every pack sold contributes to that reversal, and we track it annually.

Mukti tracks soil EC on supplier farms · published in Community Resilience Index

The Intervention
SMART has been here for twenty years

SMART's community radio station has been broadcasting in Nuh since the early 2000s — agricultural advice, health information, women's rights, community governance. The four radio volunteers who own 40% of Mukti are not a gesture. They own farmland here. Their families have grown mustard in this district for generations. They are the supply chain. They are the verification. They are the reason the claims on the pack are true.

SMART CRS licence · ~20 years in Nuh · 4 founding producer-shareholders

The Women

They grow it.
They blend it.
They own it.

Women perform approximately 70% of agricultural labour in Mewat. They sow, harvest, thresh, carry. They rarely own the land. They almost never set the price. They receive little of what it earns.

The IFAD-MADP programme (1995–2005) organised over 4,996 self-help groups in this district and ran food processing training for 2,128 women. That organisational capacity has never been converted into a commercial supply chain. Mukti is the conversion.

The entry offer is simple: a named price, paid on the day of delivery, with no middleman. Nothing about mission or equity — those come later, earned through kept promises, not asked for in advance.

The Digestive Difference

The biryani you can eat
twice a week.

Every Mukti masala is formulated on the Unani digestive tradition of Mewat — the same culinary knowledge that has been embedded in Meo cooking for centuries. The spices that prevent bloating and aid digestion are not added to the blend. They were always there. We just foregrounded them.

No mass-market masala brand — not MDH, not Everest, not Catch — makes a digestive claim. Mukti does, because it is true, because it is verifiable, and because it directly addresses why urban consumers who love biryani avoid it.

🌿
Rooted in Unani medicine
Unani tibb — the Greco-Islamic medical tradition — has a precise vocabulary for carminative (gas-reducing) herbs and spice combinations. It is the medical tradition of the Meo community. Mukti's formulation draws on that knowledge directly.
🧪
Verifiable, not a claim
The anti-flatulent action of hing, ajwain, saunf, and sonth is well-documented in clinical literature. This is not wellness marketing. It is food science that the Mewati community has practised for generations.
🏷️
On the pack, on the pitch
"Formulated on Unani digestive tradition" appears on every masala SKU. It is the differentiator that no competitor can replicate without Mewat, without the Meo community, and without twenty years of SMART's presence here.

The Six Core Carminatives

Asafoetida
hing · هینگ
Breaks down the oligosaccharides that cause gas. The most powerful anti-flatulent in Indian cooking. Already native to Mewati masalas.
Carom Seed
ajwain · اجوائن
Thymol-rich. Directly reduces bloating and intestinal spasm. Common in Mewati bread and rice dishes — familiar flavour, new claim.
Fennel Seed
saunf · سونف
Anti-spasmodic, carminative. The traditional post-meal digestive across North India. In the blend, not served alongside it.
Dried Ginger
sonth · سونٹھ
Carminative, anti-inflammatory. Reduces gas formation and aids gut motility. Part of most masala bases already — the Unani formulation just ensures the ratio is right.
Black Cardamom
badi elaichi · بڑی الائچی
Smoky, digestive, antibacterial. Foundational in Mughlai biryani. Aids gastric secretion and reduces fermentation in the gut.
Black Salt
kala namak · کالا نمک
Paradoxically aids digestion despite sulphurous notes. Stimulates bile production, reduces flatulence. Used in Unani medicine precisely for this purpose.

Ownership Structure

Who owns Mukti.
And why it matters.

A just transition company is nothing if the people most affected don't own it. Four blocks. Clean. The community holds the majority — always.

30%
SMART Founding Team

Equally distributed among SMART's founding members. Anchors long-term stewardship and continuity of mission.

Founding equity · non-dilutable
40%
Mewat Radio Volunteers

Four community radio volunteers, 10% each. They own farmland, they have networks, and they supplied the first batch. They are not staff — they are founding producers.

10% per volunteer · founding supply chain
20%
Office Staff

Two staff members who run the day-to-day operations. Their equity reflects that this company cannot function without them — and that they should benefit when it does.

Equally distributed between 2 staff members
10%
Dilution Reserve

Reserved — but conditional. A partner who receives this equity must bring market access, distribution, or a climate finance mandate. Not just capital. The community majority is structurally protected.

Climate partner · impact investor · NABARD FPO
On dilution

The 10% reserve is the only dilution window. No outside party can exceed it. Community ownership (radio volunteers) can never fall below 40% of the total. The founding team collectively holds 90% until a partner is brought in — at which point the community still holds the majority of non-SMART equity. Women producers join through a separate ESOP pool carved from future profit-sharing, not from founding equity.
